Tim Berners-Lee

In 1994 Berners-Lee leaves CERN and founds the W3C international standards organization at MIT/LCS with support from DARPA

The goal is to foster compatibility and agreement among industry members in the adoption of new standards defined by the W3C...

Tim Berners-Lee first mentioned disability access for the Web in Chicago in 1994 at the Second International Conference on the World-Wide Web.

The first standards were released the following year (also in Chicago) by engineer Gregg Vanderheiden.

At least 38 different sets of guidelines followed.

They were eventually compiled into the Unified Web Site Accessibility Guidelines.

In 1998, these guidelines were used to form the foundation of the W3C's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 1.0) with support from the White House.

2008--WCAG 2.0 released

Focus is to be technology neutral

WCAG 2.0 has since become the accepted standard

The Four POUR Principles

  1. Perceivable

    Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive.

  2. Operable

    The interface cannot require interaction that a user cannot perform.

  3. Understandable

    Make content readable and interaction predictable.

  4. Robust

    Content should remain accessible over time.

Perceivable

Guideline 1.1

Provide text alternatives for any non-text content so that it can be changed into other forms people need, such as large print, braille, speech, symbols or simpler language.

Guideline 1.2

Time-based media: Provide alternatives for time-based media.

Guideline 1.3

Create content that can be presented in different ways (for example simpler layout) without losing information or structure.

Guideline 1.4

Make it easier for users to see and hear content including separating foreground from background.

Operable

Guideline 2.1

Make all functionality available from a keyboard.

Guideline 2.2

Provide users enough time to read and use content.

Guideline 2.3

Do not design content in a way that is known to cause seizures.

Seizures

Guideline 2.4

Provide ways to help users navigate, find content, and determine where they are.

Understandable

Guideline 3.1

Make text content readable and understandable.

Guideline 3.2

Make web pages appear and operate in predictable ways.

Guideline 3.3

Help users avoid and correct mistakes.

Robust

Guideline 4.1

Maximize compatibility with current and future user agents, including assistive technologies.
